Richard Yue

NLP Engineer, CS Grad Student

Richard Yue is an NLP-focused research assistant and graduate student studying computer science at Northeastern University’s Silicon Valley Campus.

Welcome to my Github site. I’ve always been fascinated by language, from living abroad in Morocco during my undergrad years to acquiring French nationality after studying the language of Molière in Paris. I’ve made it a point to hone my multilingual communication skills over the last decade or so, which has led to an understanding of language grammar, syntax, and reasoning that has helped me master programming languages during my transition into tech. I have nearly 10 years’ experience working as a professional translator on editorial, legal, and technical texts.

With a newfound passion for computer science, I enrolled in a master’s and have been working as a graduate research assistant since September 2022, with a focus on machine learning and natural language processing. So far, I have dived headfirst into training LLMs on some interesting downstream tasks, including sentiment analysis, natural language inference, and question answering, all within the scope of multilingual text. I have also fine tuned models on novel generation tasks, most recently generating technical acronyms from long forms in order to improve technical translation through post-editing, later integrating the model into a system to fact check the resulting predictions in the scientific literature.

Other interests

An avid hiker, I have set myself the goal of tasting an espresso from every coffee roaster in the Bay Area. In my remaining time, you may find me tinkering with my Raspberry Pi or exploring yet another beach within driving distance of the Silicon Valley.
